# Product Brief

## Problem

Long notes become visually heavy as they grow. Reopening them creates anticipatory fatigue before the content is even reviewed.

Atomic notes reduce note length, but the same overwhelm can return through the visible number of notes. This suggests the issue is not only note size, but **visible accumulation**.

Traditional knowledge tools constantly expose this accumulation through sidebars, folders, graphs, counts, dashboards, and review queues. The system begins to feel like something that must be managed.

## Insight

The user does not need less knowledge. They need less awareness of its total scale while thinking.

Knowledge should remain available without being continuously visible.

## Product Direction

Build a calm, local-first notes app that reveals only what is relevant to the current thought.

The app begins with a blank composer. Typing either retrieves related notes or creates a new one. Search and backlinks provide access to existing knowledge without exposing the full collection.

## Core Principle

> Remember everything without showing everything.

The product should feel like beginning a thought, not entering a growing archive.

## Success

The experiment succeeds if users experience:

* Less resistance when opening the app
* Less overwhelm when returning to old knowledge
* Easier retrieval without browsing the collection
* More focus on learning than maintaining a system
